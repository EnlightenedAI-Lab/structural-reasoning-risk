CUBICS Assurance

CUBICS Assurance is a deterministic audit instrument that checks whether a document, on its own, contains sufficient evidence to structurally support the claims it makes before a decision is taken.

Instrument Overview

CUBICS Assurance is a structural reasoning audit instrument designed to evaluate the decision-readiness of documentation. It analyzes whether a submitted artifact (such as a Key Information Document, dossier, or deck) contains sufficient internal evidence to support its claims without requiring external assumptions.

Primary Question

"Does this document, evaluated in isolation, contain a structurally complete and auditable chain of reasoning from stated evidence to asserted conclusions?"

NOTE: Current analyses may include manual or semi-automated review steps. Full automation status is disclosed per artifact.

Assurance Modules

Module A

Claim–Evidence Consistency

Scans document submissions to map every affirmative claim to its supporting premise. Detects unsupported assertions and logical gaps.

Module B

Governance & Accountability

Validates the presence of required audit trails, accountable owners, and verifiable timestamps for key decisions.

Module C

Risk & Integrity Signals

Analyzes semantic patterns to detect overconfidence, "marketing fluff" disguised as fact, and missing substantiation.

Assurance Signals

Format Context: Pitch Decks & Summary Presentations
Pitch decks are designed for persuasion and orientation, not comprehensive auditability. High rates of UNSUPPORTED or WEAK signals are an expected baseline characteristic of this format. In this context, the instrument functions to generate a "Diligence Question List"—identifying exactly which high-confidence claims require external verification.
Signal Meaning What failure looks like
structural_coherence Degree to which conclusions logically follow from stated premises. Conclusions that "sound right" but lack evidentiary steps.
premise_coverage Percentage of distinct claims supported by at least one piece of evidence. A deck full of assertions with no reference citations.
orphan_rate Volume of claims that have zero structural support links. "We will dominate the market" (orphaned from market data).
evidence_gap_risk Composite probability that a statement is fabricated or lacks source documents. Specific numbers or dates that appear without source documents.
overconfidence_gap Distance between the certainty of language and the strength of evidence. Using words like "guaranteed" or "proven" on weak premises.

Cost

Minimizes billable hours spent on basic structural assurance cycles.

Risk

System Workflow

System Inputs
  • KID PDF / Word Documents
  • Supporting Annexes (Market study, Capex sheets)
  • Engineering Notes & Technical Claims
  • Claims Register (Auto-generated or Manual)
Assurance Outputs
  • 1-Page Assurance Summary (PASS/WARN/FAIL)
  • Claim–Evidence Map (CSV + Viewer)
  • Governance Gap List (Missing Owners)
  • Audit Log Bundle (Hashes + Timestamps)
Assurance Status is non-binding but auditable; final approval remains with the Council of Sages.

Scope & Limitations: This analysis evaluates the structural integrity of the submitted artifact in isolation. It validates whether claims are connected to stated premises within the file. It does NOT verify factual truth against external reality, assess the intent of authors, or predict project success. The instrument is a risk-diagnostic tool intended to expose reliance on unverified assumptions within the decision loop.
