Methodology

Structural Assurance is a structural validation protocol applied to investor-facing documents (Key Information Decks, pitch decks, technical dossiers). It does not evaluate the quality of an idea, technology, or organization. It evaluates whether claims are sufficiently evidenced, owned, and auditable to proceed through a governance or investment process.

  1. Claim Extraction: All affirmative claims are identified. A claim is defined as any statement asserting an outcome, capability, impact, performance, or future state.
  2. Evidence Mapping: Each claim is traced to explicit supporting material within the document or its referenced annexes.
  3. Structural Signal Evaluation: Claims and their evidence links are evaluated across a fixed set of assurance signals.
  4. Governance & Auditability Check: Presence of accountable owners, timestamps, and traceable responsibility is assessed.

Signal Computation

Each assurance signal is computed from normalized structural observations rather than subjective scoring.

  • Structural Coherence: Measures whether conclusions logically follow from stated premises. Penalized when outcomes are asserted without intermediate reasoning steps.
  • Evidence Coverage: Percentage of affirmative claims that have at least one explicit, document-local evidence anchor.
  • Orphan Rate: Percentage of claims with zero supporting evidence or accountable ownership.
  • Overconfidence Gap: Measures mismatch between linguistic certainty (e.g. “will”, “guaranteed”) and the strength of evidence.

Thresholds reflect governance readiness standards, not project merit.

What This Instrument Does Not Do

  • It does not rank or compare projects.
  • It does not assess technological feasibility or commercial viability.
  • It does not replace expert judgment, Council review, or regulatory approval.

Structural Assurance functions as a gate, not a verdict.
