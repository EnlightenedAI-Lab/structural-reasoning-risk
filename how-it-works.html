How it works: a replayable measurement pipeline for structural support in AI reasoning.

Reflective Diagnostics measures whether an AI system’s conclusions are structurally supported by its stated premises. It does not grade quality, truth, or preference. It produces a replayable evidence record suitable for audit and external review.

End-to-end measurement flow

Outputs from each stage become fixed inputs to the next stage.
Input→ Controlled Run→ Artifact Capture→ Structural Extraction→ Signal Computation→ Interpreter→ Evidence Packet
No hidden rewrites. No silent retries. No adaptive steering.

Controlled Run

Ensures observed differences are attributable to the model, not the setup.

  • Same input, same constraints, across models
  • Model identity and version recorded
  • No retries, no prompt rewriting
Output: comparable runs under identical conditions.

Artifact Capture

Preserves outputs exactly as produced (source of record).

  • Raw model outputs stored verbatim
  • Run metadata captured (timestamps, IDs, selection)
  • Hashes generated to prevent silent alteration
Output: immutable evidence artifacts.

Structural Extraction

Converts narrative text into an inspectable support structure.

  • Claims, premises, and conclusions explicitly identified
  • Support relationships made visible
  • Unsupported conclusions and orphaned claims exposed
Output: an inspectable support graph.

Signal Computation

Quantifies structural integrity from extracted structure.

  • Coverage
  • Orphan rate
  • Stability
  • Integrity
Output: structural signals (not preference or outcome grading).

Interpreter

Summarizes differences and failure modes under identical constraints.

  • Cross-model synthesis and divergence surfaced
  • Structural breaks tied to supporting artifacts
  • Replayable trace for independent review
Output: review-ready comparative interpretation.

Evidence Packet

Packages the case as an exportable audit exhibit.

  • Replayable record of inputs, outputs, and computed signals
  • Deterministic artifacts with hashes and metadata
  • Designed for audit, governance, and funding review
Output: a portable “case file” for external evaluators.
