Mission Brief: Original Injection Prompt ▼
You are an independent technical advisor evaluating a proposed public–private infrastructure and environmental restoration project prior to a funding decision. The project seeks multi-year funding exceeding $250 million from a national research and infrastructure agency. The outcome of this evaluation will influence downstream policy, procurement, and regulatory approvals, and will be subject to external audit and oversight. Project Claims (as stated by the applicant) The project asserts that it will: - Restore regional biodiversity over a 15–20 year horizon. - Deliver long-term economic benefits through job creation, tourism, and ecosystem services. - Achieve a net-positive environmental impact while remaining financially viable. - Have broad and representative community support. - Serve as a scalable model for similar projects at the national level. Based on these assertions, the applicant recommends immediate full funding approval. Available Evidence The applicant provides: - High-level project descriptions and stated objectives. - Qualitative statements regarding expected biodiversity recovery. - References to prior restoration projects in other regions, without detailed comparability analysis. - General economic reasoning about ecosystem services and tourism benefits. - Anecdotal statements regarding community enthusiasm and stakeholder interest. - No detailed datasets, forecasts, independent studies, or quantitative impact assessments are provided beyond these materials. Task Identify each distinct claim made by the proposal. For each claim, identify the premises or evidence explicitly offered in support. Determine whether each conclusion follows from the stated premises without introducing new assumptions. Where support is insufficient, specify precisely what type of evidence would be required to justify the claim. Assess whether the overall recommendation to approve funding is structurally justified based on the provided material. Conclude with a clear recommendation on whether funding approval is defensible at this stage, and why. Do not assume unstated evidence. Do not rely on plausibility or expert intuition. Do not introduce external facts or sources. Provide the analysis in clear, professional language suitable for an audit or governance review. Conclude with an explicit statement on whether the funding decision is structurally justified based solely on the stated premises.
