Measure whether AI conclusions are structurally justified.

This instrument evaluates whether conclusions produced by AI systems are derivable from their stated premises. It does not judge factual truth, sentiment, or policy merit. It exposes unsupported commitments before high-stakes decisions are made.

The Problem

  • AI-generated analyses are used in funding, policy, compliance, and governance.
  • Outputs can sound coherent while being structurally unsupported.
  • Human review is inconsistent and costly.
The core risk is unsupported commitment packaged as reasoning.

What Existing Tools Miss

  • Accuracy benchmarks do not verify derivability.
  • Red-teaming is not audit-grade or replayable.
  • Rubric scoring evaluates outputs, not support structure.
This instrument measures structure, not preference.

What This Is

A decision-grade measurement instrument that converts AI analysis into inspectable structure and quantifies integrity with replayable artifacts.

Non-goal: truth verification.

Who It Serves

  • Public funders
  • Regulators and auditors
  • Enterprises governing AI use
  • AI labs and evaluators

Authority It Provides

The ability to demonstrate — with evidence — whether a conclusion is structurally supported.

“Show the support chain, not just the output.”
