Why This Format

Decision-grade governance requires portable, inspectable records—not interactive dashboards.

Definition: Audit Artifact

A self-contained, static record designed for archiving, forwarding, and independent review (board, legal, regulator) without requiring a live application.

THE DESIGN CHOICE

Most software tools optimize for interaction. CUBICS optimizes for accountability.

Dashboards are designed to be explored. Audit artifacts are designed to be preserved, shared, and defended.

WHY DASHBOARDS FAIL IN GOVERNANCE

  • Dashboards change over time; audit records must not.
  • Dashboards depend on live systems; audits must survive system failure.
  • Dashboards obscure decision context; audits must freeze it.
  • Dashboards often depend on vendor runtime; audit records must remain readable without it.
If a regulator cannot open the result years later without a live platform, it is not decision-grade.
The Output

WHAT CUBICS PRODUCES INSTEAD

Each CUBICS run produces a self-contained HTML audit artifact that includes:

  • The evaluated document reference (source + date)
  • The detected claims and their support structure
  • The applied rules and thresholds
  • The resulting structural verdict
  • A complete claim–evidence trace

The file requires no backend, no database, and no proprietary runtime.

WHY THIS FORMAT MATTERS

  • Regulatory defensibility
  • Long-term archival and chain-of-custody
  • Vendor-independent review
  • Faster board / legal diligence

This is why CUBICS outputs resemble legal exhibits rather than software dashboards.

The artifact is the record.
Scope Boundary

WHAT CUBICS IS NOT

  • Not a writing assistant
  • Not a scoring toy
  • Not a live monitoring dashboard
  • Not a black-box AI verdict

CUBICS is a structural assurance instrument that produces inspectable decision artifacts. It surfaces missing support so decisions don’t quietly depend on unverified assumptions.
