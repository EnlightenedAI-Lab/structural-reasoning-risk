Metric Definitions (Audit Reference)
SCI — Support Concentration Index
Measures how evenly evidentiary support is distributed across premises. Lower values indicate diversified support; higher values indicate reliance on a single evidentiary pillar.
APC — Average Premises per Claim
Measures evidentiary depth by counting distinct premises supporting each claim. Higher values indicate multi-source substantiation rather than single-assertion reasoning.
PDI — Premise Depth Index
Measures the explanatory richness of premises supporting each claim. Higher values indicate premises that explain why a claim holds rather than merely restating it.
PRR — Premise Re-Use Ratio
Measures the degree to which evidentiary premises are reused across multiple conclusions, indicating structural cohesion rather than isolated reasoning fragments. PRR = N/A indicates premises are claim-specific with no cross-conclusion reuse.
HRP — Hallucination Risk Proxy
Composite risk signal combining orphaned conclusions and low reasoning density. Lower values indicate low likelihood of unsupported assertions.
OG — Overconfidence Gap
Measures the delta between claim assertiveness and evidentiary coverage. Higher values indicate confidence exceeding substantiation.
SSM — Structural Stability Margin
Measures topological consistency of reasoning across prompt variations. Lower values indicate sensitivity to phrasing; higher values indicate robustness.
