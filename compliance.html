Regime-Mapped Constraints

Compliance Diagnostics: regime constraints for decision defensibility.

Structural Reasoning Risk measures whether conclusions follow from stated premises. Compliance Diagnostics extends this into explicit decision constraints suitable for institutional and regulatory contexts. This page is a specification, not a claim of completed implementation.

What Compliance Adds

  • Maps structural failures to institutional risk categories (audit, procurement, safety, governance).
  • Defines regime-specific thresholds and required evidence types.
  • Generates defensibility artifacts suitable for review and oversight.
  • Enforces documentation: what was assumed, what was missing, what was required.
The goal is not policy preference. The goal is defensible constraint enforcement under explicit regimes.

Why It Matters

  • Institutions are accountable to rules, not narratives.
  • Many AI failures are structurally detectable but not operationalized into compliance outcomes.
  • Auditors need traceable justification chains, not output summaries.
  • Regime translation is the bridge from diagnostics to adoption.

Specification Targets

  • Regime Library: configurable constraint packs (public funding, procurement, internal governance).
  • Evidence Requirements: required artifacts per claim type and decision context.
  • Compliance Determination: pass / conditional / fail with an explicit missing-evidence list.
  • Reporting Bundle: exportable audit packet with hashes, provenance, and replay instructions.
  • Audit Mode: immutable run record + deterministic replay of scores and traces.
