Regime Mapped Constraints

Compliance Diagnostics turns structural reasoning risk into enforceable regime constraints.

Structural Reasoning Risk measures whether conclusions follow from stated premises. Compliance Diagnostics extends this into formal decision constraints for institutional and regulatory contexts. This page is a specification, not a claim of completed implementation.

What Compliance Adds

  • Maps structural failures to institutional risk categories (audit, procurement, safety, governance).
  • Defines regime specific thresholds and required evidence types.
  • Generates decision defensibility artifacts suitable for review and oversight.
  • Enforces documentation: what was assumed, what was missing, what was required.
The goal is not policy preference. The goal is defensible constraint enforcement under explicit regimes.

Why It Matters

  • Institutions are accountable to rules, not narratives.
  • Many AI failures are structurally detectable but not operationalized into compliance decisions.
  • Auditors need traceable justification chains, not output summaries.
  • Regime translation is the bridge from diagnostics to adoption.

Planned Capabilities

  • Regime Library: configurable constraints per domain (public funding, internal governance, procurement).
  • Evidence Requirements: required artifacts per claim type and decision context.
  • Compliance Determination: pass, conditional, fail, with explicit missing evidence list.
  • Reporting: court grade export bundle with hashes, provenance, and replay instructions.
  • Audit Mode: immutable run record and deterministic replay of scores.
