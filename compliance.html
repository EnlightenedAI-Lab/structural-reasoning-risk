Compliance Diagnostics converts structural reasoning risk into enforceable regime constraints.

Structural Reasoning Risk measures whether conclusions follow from stated premises. Compliance Diagnostics extends those measurements into explicit decision constraints suitable for institutional and regulatory contexts. This page is a specification of intended behavior — implementation is incremental and evidence-led.

The goal is not policy preference. The goal is defensible constraint enforcement under explicit regimes.

What Compliance Adds

  • Regime mapping: translate failures into categories (audit, procurement, safety, governance).
  • Constraint rules: regime-specific thresholds and required evidence types.
  • Decision defensibility: artifacts designed for oversight review and challenge.
  • Documentation enforcement: what was assumed, what was missing, what was required.

Why It Matters

  • Institutions are accountable to rules, not narratives.
  • Structural failures are often detectable but not operationalized into compliance outcomes.
  • Auditors need traceable justification chains — not summaries.
  • Regime translation is the bridge from diagnostics to adoption.

Specification Targets

  • Regime Library: configurable constraint packs (public funding, procurement, internal governance).
  • Evidence Requirements: required artifacts per claim type and decision context.
  • Compliance Determination: pass / conditional / fail with an explicit missing-evidence list.
  • Reporting Bundle: exportable audit packet with hashes, provenance, and replay instructions.
  • Audit Mode: immutable run record + deterministic replay of scores and traces.

Outputs Produced

  • Constraint Sheet: regime rules triggered and thresholds applied.
  • Missing Evidence List: required artifacts not present in the prompt/evidence packet.
  • Defensibility Note: one-page rationale suitable for oversight review.
  • Replay Header: model IDs, run hash, and reproduction reference.

Non-Claims

  • Does not determine moral value or policy desirability.
  • Does not certify factual truth of external claims.
  • Does not replace legal counsel or statutory interpretation.
  • It enforces structural and regime constraints on what can be justified from provided evidence.
