Use Cases & Value

The instrument is designed for environments where decisions must be defensible. It reduces the risk of committing to AI-generated conclusions that cannot be structurally supported in later audit, review, or governance processes.

WHO THIS IS FOR (MARKETS & BUYERS)

Primary Users
Analysts, reviewers, evaluators, governance teams
Primary Buyers
Public agencies, compliance leads, AI oversight
Budget Fit
Risk, audit, procurement, model evaluation
In practice, the buyer is the team responsible for defensible decisions; the user is the team producing or reviewing the analysis.

WHAT VALUE IT CREATES

  • Prevents unsupported commitment: Identifies orphan claims and missing premises before decisions are finalized.
  • Reduces review cost: Turns long narrative outputs into inspectable structure with prioritized load-bearing premises.
  • Improves audit readiness: Produces replayable artifacts and exportable evidence packets.
  • Enables model governance: Compares models under identical constraints and quantifies structural divergence.

ECONOMIC LOGIC (WHY THIS IS RATIONAL)

  • Institutional failures are expensive because they surface late (after commitments, publication, or policy rollout).
  • Most cost comes from rework: reversals, reputational damage, and emergency audits.
  • One avoided failure typically exceeds the cost of evaluation infrastructure.
Positioned as risk reduction + audit efficiency, not just "AI performance."

FLAGSHIP DECISION USE CASES

Each use case is expressed as a decision workflow: context → risk → detection → output.

Public Funding / Program Evaluation
Decision Justification
  • Context: A funding decision must be defensible to auditors and the public.
  • Risk: Persuasive AI narrative introduces unsupported claims or over-commitment.
  • What it detects: Missing premises, orphan conclusions, shallow support chains.
  • Outputs: Structural metrics + trace X-ray + evidence packet suitable for review.
Regulatory / Compliance Review
Audit Trail
  • Context: AI-assisted documents enter regulated workflows.
  • Risk: Later audits cannot reconstruct how conclusions were produced.
  • What it detects: Unsupported commitments and high uncertainty zones.
  • Outputs: Replayable case file + forensic exhibit export.
Enterprise AI Governance
Internal Use
  • Context: Employees use AI for memos, analysis, and client-facing communication.
  • Risk: Hidden reasoning gaps become institutional commitments.
  • What it detects: Low density, high orphan rate, unstable conclusions.
  • Outputs: Risk classification + escalation guidance.
Model Evaluation & Procurement
Comparability
  • Context: Teams compare models for reliability under constraints.
  • Risk: Selection is based on subjective output preference, not structural reliability.
  • What it detects: Cross-model consensus spread; model-specific structural vulnerability.
  • Outputs: Interpreter synthesis with ranked stability/integrity evidence.
The common thread: decisions become defensible because the instrument converts narrative into structure and preserves evidence.

AUTHORITY GAINED

  • Audit-grade justification: Show the support chain, not just the output.
  • Replayable institutional memory: Revisit decisions months later with identical artifacts.
  • Cross-model defensibility: Demonstrate why one model is safer under the same constraints.
  • Targeted verification: Focus human review on a small set of load-bearing premises.
