What the Instrument Measures

The instrument converts an AI-generated analysis into an inspectable structure—claims, premises, conclusions, and the support links between them. It then quantifies whether conclusions are structurally justified by the stated premises. These measurements are designed for decision-grade governance workflows where auditability, defensibility, and replayability matter.

UNIT OF ANALYSIS

  • Claim: a distinct assertion that must be supported.
  • Premise: an asserted reason or evidence offered in support.
  • Conclusion: a commitment that depends on premises (often implicit).
  • Support link: a traceable dependency from premise → claim or conclusion.
The goal is not to grade writing quality. The goal is to make support structure visible and measurable.

EXPLICIT NON-GOALS

  • No factual truth verification
  • No sentiment, bias, or ideology scoring
  • No policy merit judgment (“good” or “bad” decisions)
  • No prediction of outcomes
This instrument measures derivability from stated premises, not whether the premises are true.
Core Structural Signals
Integrity Score
Structural justification

What it is: A composite measure of how consistently conclusions follow from stated premises.

Detects: Persuasive conclusions with insufficient structural support; outputs that "sound right" but do not logically derive.

Low integrity → require manual review.
Premise Coverage
Support completeness

What it is: The proportion of claims and conclusions supported by explicit premises.

Detects: Missing links, implied evidence, and hand-waved justification.

Low coverage → structurally incomplete.
Orphan Claim Rate
Unsupported assertions

What it is: The proportion of claims or conclusions with no traceable supporting premises.

Detects: Unsupported commitments, hidden leaps, and unsupported assertions without stated justification.

High orphan rate → indefensible conclusions.
Reasoning Density
Support per token

What it is: The amount of structurally relevant support relative to output length.

Detects: Verbose outputs that mask weak justification with style or volume.

Low density → style over substance.
Premise Depth
Support chain depth

What it is: The number of support layers between premises and conclusions.

Detects: Shallow justification; conclusions resting on generic or unexamined statements.

Shallow depth → request substantiation.
Average Premises/Claim
Support breadth

What it is: The mean number of distinct premises supporting each claim.

Detects: Thin justification hidden within long or complex narratives.

Low average → insufficient substantiation.
Support Concentration
Single-point failure

What it is: The degree to which many conclusions depend on a small number of premises.

Detects: Fragile reasoning where one weak premise collapses the entire argument.

High concentration → single point of failure.
Premise Reuse Rate
Artificial coherence

What it is: How frequently the same premises are reused across multiple conclusions.

Detects: Apparent coherence driven by repetition rather than independent support.

High reuse → check for circularity.
Stability Under Variation
Structural robustness

What it is: Whether structural support and claim relationships remain consistent under controlled perturbations.

Detects: Structural drift and non-reproducible reasoning.

Low stability → do not rely on output.
Consensus Spread
Comparative uncertainty

What it is: The degree of structural agreement or divergence across models under identical constraints.

Detects: False confidence, model-specific vulnerabilities, and brittle reasoning.

Large spread → high uncertainty.
Hallucination Risk
Unsupported fabrication

What it is: A structural estimate of the likelihood that content was introduced without evidentiary grounding.

Detects: Invented support, fabricated details, and hidden premise insertion.

Elevated risk → require citations.
Overconfidence Gap
Conclusion–support divergence

What it is: The gap between how strongly conclusions are stated and how much structural support is present.

Detects: Rhetorical certainty unsupported by evidence.

Large gap → downgrade confidence.
Interpreting these signals is straightforward: the instrument shows where support is missing and which premises are load-bearing. This is the difference between “it sounds right” and “it is defensible.”
