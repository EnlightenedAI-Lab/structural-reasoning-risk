This is not “AI evaluation.” It is a failure-prevention instrument for decisions made under audit, legal, or public scrutiny. It evaluates whether a conclusion can be defended from stated premises — not whether it sounds persuasive.

INSTRUMENT: STRUCTURAL REASONING AUDIT
OUTPUT: DEFENSIBILITY SIGNAL
RISK CLASS: DECISION UNDER SCRUTINY
Core framing shift
This instrument does not evaluate what an AI says — it evaluates whether a decision can survive being questioned.

Vertical markets (buyers + use cases)
Public sector & government
Who: ministries · regulators · municipalities · procurement bodies
Why they care

AI-generated memos increasingly appear in funding, policy, and planning documents. Audit failures become reputational and political failures.

What this prevents
  • Funding approvals justified by narrative confidence rather than evidence
  • Post-hoc audit findings that no one can trace or defend
Finance, investment & capital allocation
Who: funds · ESG committees · foundations · grant-makers
Why they care

AI is used for screening, summaries, and diligence. “AI-assisted diligence” increases exposure when rationales cannot be defended.

What this prevents
  • False confidence in investment rationales
  • Inability to prove diligence was structurally sound
Legal, compliance & risk
Who: law firms · in-house · compliance · risk committees
Why they care

AI reasoning is entering briefs, compliance reviews, and internal memos. Courts and regulators care about justification structure.

What this prevents
  • Hallucinations dressed as legal reasoning
  • Untraceable chains of justification in filings
Journalism, investigations & oversight
Who: journalists · watchdog NGOs · committees · think tanks
Why they care

AI summaries are used as research accelerants. Narrative confidence can mask evidentiary emptiness.

What this prevents
  • Publishing analyses that collapse under scrutiny
  • Repeating claims that appear sourced but are structurally unsupported
Corporate strategy & board-level decisions
Who: executives · boards · internal audit
Why they care

AI-generated strategy decks and memos are circulating in boardrooms. Accountability remains with humans even if AI was used.

What this prevents
  • Strategic decisions justified by polished but hollow reasoning
  • Post-incident inability to explain why a decision was taken
What this instrument detects that standard evaluations miss
Zero-support reasoning
Reasoning that sounds justified but contains no evidentiary scaffolding.
Coverage illusions
Claims enumerated without being justified — structure implies sufficiency when none exists.
Stability failures
Conclusions shift under minor rephrasing, indicating brittleness and decision fragility.
Structural hallucinations
Unsupported commitments that evade content-based checks because tone appears “professional.”
Compliance theatre
Documents that look audit-ready but cannot be defended when questioned line-by-line.
Describing vs performing diligence
Distinguishes “what evidence would be needed” from “what is actually supported by provided evidence.”
Addressable use cases
The Structural Reasoning Audit is designed for environments where AI-generated analysis must withstand external scrutiny. It applies to public funding approvals, investment and grant committees, regulatory and compliance reviews, legal and investigative workflows, and board-level strategic decisions. In these environments, the primary risk is not incorrect facts, but structurally unjustified conclusions presented with persuasive confidence.
